# Lesson 4: それではまず、行きかたについてご説明します。

#4
Offering explanations using photos and other materials
それではまず、行きかたについてご説明します。
First, I&apos;ll explain how to get there.
では、ミャンマーに詳しいものからご説明します。
Our expert will explain about Myanmar.
「では」is a phrase used when changing the subject.
It&apos;s a shortened version of「それでは」.
By adding the honorific「ご」in front of 「説明します」to create「ご説明します」, the phrase becomes more polite.
じゃあ、お願いします。
OK, then please go ahead.
「じゃあ」is a casual form of「では」. It&apos;s appropriate for use from superiors to subordinates, or among equals.
はい、わかりました。
Understood.
それではまず、行きかたについてご説明します。
First, I&apos;ll explain how to get there.
「それでは」is a phrase that responds to the previous speaker&apos;s words, and precedes a reaction. In this case, the current speaker is responding to「お願いします」.
This phrase is also used before making an explanation.

「こちらの」is a more polite way to say「この」.
「ご覧ください」is a more polite way to say「見てください」.
インレー湖はヤンゴンから飛行機で約１時間です。
Lake Inle is about one hour from Yangon by plane.
This phrase is useful for sharing how much time it takes to reach a destination.
You can subtitute「電車で」(by train)「車で」(by car)「タクシーで」(by taxi) instead of 「飛行機で」to show how to get there by different modes of transportation.
For &quot;on foot&quot; use「歩くと」.
インレー湖 (Lake Inle) is the name of a lake. The character 湖 can be read as 「みずうみ 」or 「こ」.
琵琶湖（びわこ） Lake Biwa
芦ノ湖（あしのこ）Lake Ashi (also called: Lake Ashi-no-ko)
あ、１時間ですか。
Ah, one hour?
The listener is checking what the speaker said.
Adding「あ」in the beginning shows the listener&apos;s reaction to what they have just heard.
To express disappointment, a long「あー」with falling intonation is used.
次に、インレー湖の見どころについてご説明します。
Next, I&apos;ll explain the highlights of Lake Inle.
Using phrases like まず and 次にto connect phrases allows the listener to easily follow what is being said.
「見どころ」means highlight. It can be used in cases like this, such as explaining the must-see sights at a destination.
「見どころ」 can also be used to mean &quot;something to look out for&quot; in reference to things like sports or movies.
ここに住む人たちは湖の上で生活をしています。
The people who live here carry out their daily lives on the water.
「ここに住む」modifies「人たち」. See the below examples.
「渋谷に来る人たち」→「渋谷に来る」「人たち」 the people who come to Shibuya
「先に食べる人たち」→「先に食べる」「人たち」 the people who eat first
「電車で帰る人たち」→「電車で帰る」「人たち」 the people who go home by train
それを見ることができます。
You can see that.
1年間に16万人以上の観光客が訪れます。
Over 160,000 tourists visit each year.
This is a useful phrase to describe a bustling tourist spot.
「訪れます」(visit) uses the present tense to communicate that people regularly visit this spot.
For the past tense, use:
「昨年は16万人以上の観光客が訪れました。」 Last year, 160,000 tourists visited here.
To predict the future, use:
「１年間に16万人以上の観光客が見込まれます。」It&apos;s expected that 160,000 tourists will visit here yearly.
へえ、そうなんですね。
Wow, is that so?!
へえis a casual phrase, with a similar meaning to「ああ」.
Phrases like 「そうなんですね」communicate surprise and acknowledgment. It&apos;s a common spoken phrase.
インレー湖は日本人観光客にも人気が出ますかね。
Do many Japanese tourists visit Lake Inle?
The phrase「人気が出ますかね」, like 「人気が出るかな？」shows that the speaker is wondering if this is the case or not. This phrase is more polite than「かな」and gently asks for the listener&apos;s agreement.
By adding 「も」 onto 「日本人の観光客にも」includes the idea that the spot is already popular with tourists from other countries and regions.
はい、日本の方も楽しめると思います。
Yes, I think Japanese people would really enjoy it.
「～と思います」 is a phrase commonly used to express one&apos;s opinion.
そうですか。検討します
I see. Please give me some time to consider.
「検討します」means consider, but is not a guarantee that the speaker will enter business negotiations.
「前向きに検討します」(I&apos;ll consider it positively.) may indicate a better chance!

「まず」has a similar meaning to「はじめに」「最初に」. It&apos;s a phrase that can be used to start off an explanation.
To make explanations that are easy for listeners to understand, it&apos;s important to use words that indicate order, such as
「まず」 First,
「次に」 Next,
「最後に」 Last,
This will help the listener to know what&apos;s coming next.
検討 「検討」
consideration
「検討」means to consider something.
It&apos;s often used in the workplace when an idea is being considered by multiple staff members at a meeting.
There is no guarantee that 「検討」 will lead to a positive response.
「前向きに検討します」may indicate more strongly that a response will be positive.
前向きに検討します。　「まえむきけんとうします」　I will consider it positively.
社内でもう一度検討します。　「しゃないでもういちどけんとうします」
Please allow us to reconsider internally.
上司と検討してからまたご連絡します。　「じょうしとけんとうしてからごれんらくします」
I&apos;ll consult with my superior and contact you later.
