# Lesson 8: よろしければ、私が致しましょうか。

#8
Making suggestions
よろしければ、私が致しましょうか。
If you don&apos;t mind, shall I do that?
どうですか。進んでいますか。
Everything going okay?
Here, this phrase is being used to check progress. 「進んでいますか」 is used here to mean 「仕事は進んでいますか」 (How is work going?). In this case, since both the speaker and listener know the situation, there is no need to say「仕事は」.
はい、お客様。もう終わりそうです。
Yes, ma&apos;am. I&apos;m nearly finished.
The speaker is responding politely. 「もう終わりそうです」means &quot;nearly finished, but not finished yet.&quot;
「終わりそう」uses the verb 終わる and そうです as follows:
終わり&lt;ます&gt;＋そうです→終わりそうです。
This form can be used with other verbs as well:
動詞&lt;ます形のstem&gt;+そうです
Ex. この仕事はあと30分で終わりそうです → I&apos;ll finish this task in half an hour.
この店はもうすぐ閉まりそうです（閉まります+そうです） → This store is about to close.
あら、いつも早くて助かるわ。ありがとう。
You&apos;re always so fast! Thank you.
「あら、」is an expression of mild surprise, often used by more elderly women. The 「わ」in「早くて助かるわ」is a feminine way to end a sentence. Men may tend to use the following expression instead:「あ、いつも早くて助かるよ。ありがとう」
どういたしまして。
You&apos;re welcome.
This is a response to 「ありがとう」. It&apos;s a comparatively friendly response. To be more polite, use a humble phrase like 「いえいえ、そんなことありません」.
あそこに戻さないと。
I&apos;d better put it back over there.
「戻さないと」is a shortened form of「戻さないといけません」. In this case, the speaker is talking to herself.
あっ！痛たたたたた。腰が。
Ah! Ow-ow-ow! My back!
This is an expression used when you&apos;ve suddenly hurt yourself. 「あっ！」is an expression of surprise. 「いたたたた」is used to mean &quot;ouch.&quot; You can use this phrase for any part of your body, not just your back.
大丈夫ですか。
Are you alright?
This phrase is often used to express concern.
よろしければ、私が致しましょうか。
If you don&apos;t mind, shall I do that?
This phrase is being used to make a polite offer of help. In this form, the phrase is appropriate for use with superiors or clients. To be more casual, use the following:「よければ、私がしましょうか。」or「よければ、私がしようか」. In the workplace, it&apos;s important to quickly make offers of help when you think help may be needed. If you see someone in need, try using this phrase to offer help!

あぁ、お願いします。　Oh, thank you.
Here,「あぁ」has a nuance of「いいですか。」. This is a casual expression.
この本の片付けも、よろしければ、私が致しましょうか。　Shall I tidy up these books, too?
お願いします。ありがとう。　Yes, please. Thank you.
Words from this Dialogue
お客様「おきゃくさま」
customer / client
「お客様」and「お客さん」are both words used to mean &quot;customer&quot; or &quot;client.&quot;
「お客様」is more formal, and is used by people in the service industry, when directly speaking with a customer.
「お客さん」is used in more friendly situations, such as when the customer is someone you have seen before. It may be more commonly used at small businesses and retail shops.
「顧客」is another word for &quot;customer&quot; or &quot;client,&quot; but is not used when speaking directly to a customer.
