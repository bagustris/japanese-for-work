# Lesson 20: 場合によっては間に合わないこともあります。よろしいでしょうか。

#20
Telling passengers in a hurry that they may be late
場合によっては間に合わないこともあります。よろしいでしょうか。
We may not make it in time. Will that be okay?
運転手さん、予約した和田です。
Driver, I&apos;m Wada. I made the booking.
「運転手さん」is being used here to call out to the driver. In many situations, you can add 「さん」after a profession in this way.
Ex.「駅員さん」station worker /「美容師さん」hair stylist /「看護師さん」nurse /「パン屋さん」baker
お待ちしておりました。
I&apos;ve been waiting for you.
「お待ちしておりました」is a polite way of saying「待っていました」. This is not meant to point out that someone was late. Instead, it is meant to say that someone was expected and welcome. It&apos;s a form of customer service おもてなし.
この荷物、後ろに入れてもらえますか。
Can you put this in the trunk?
The phrase above omits the particle 「を」from the sentence 「この荷物を後ろに入れてもらえますか」. In relaxed conversation, particles are often omitted in this way. 「～てもらえますか」is used to ask someone to do something politely.
お預かりします。
Certainly.
This is a polite way of saying「荷物を預かります」.
あっ、新幹線に間に合うかな。乗ろう！
Will we make the Shinkansen? Let&apos;s go.
「あっ」is an expression used when remembering something. In this case, the speaker is recalling that they need to make the Shinkansen, and is worried that they may not make it in time. The form「動詞（辞書形）＋かな」expresses doubt, and is often used in casual conversation. 「乗ろう」is a casual form of「乗りましょう」.
ドアをお閉めします。ご注意ください。
I&apos;ll close the door now. Please be careful.
「お閉めします」is a humble form of 閉めます, to close. 「ご注意ください」is a respectful form of 「注意してください」. Use the following formula: 「ご＋名詞（注意）＋ください」. You often hear phrases using this construction on public transport.
１４時の新幹線に乗りたいんです。
We need to make the 2 PM train.
「～んです」 is used to add emphasis to the situation. In this case, the situation is: 「14時の新幹線に乗りたいです。あまり時間がありませんね。間に合うかな、大丈夫かな。」.
In Japan, the 24-hour clock is often used when referring to time, even in conversation.
急いで東京駅までお願いします。
Please hurry to Tokyo Station.
「急いで～までお願いします」is a useful phrase that can be used to relay to the driver that one is in a hurry. It means the same thing as 「東京駅まで。急いでください。」.
かしこまりました。ナビでお調べいたします。
Certainly. I will check the navigation.
「かしこまりました」is a more polite form of「わかりました」. It can be used in business when speaking to a superior, or at a restaurant when speaking to a customer. The 「ナビ」in 「ナビでお調べいたします」is short for「ナビゲーション」, or GPS system. 「お調べいたします」is a polite form of 「調べます」. Use the following formula: 「お＋調べ（ます形）＋いたします」.
大丈夫ですか。あと３０分で行けますか。
I see.
「行けます」is the potential form of 「行きます」.「大丈夫ですか」is being used to ask whether, based on the current situation, they will be able to make it in time.「あと30分で行けますか」is asking whether or not they will make it within 30 minutes.
通常は３０分で行けますが、道路事情によって変わります。
Normally it takes 30 minutes, but it depends on the traffic conditions.
「通常は」means the same thing as「いつもは」and「普段は」. 「道路事情によって変わります」means that, depe

えっ、指定席とっているんですけど。
Oh, no! We have reserved tickets.
「えっ」is an expression of surprise. Here, 「指定席をとっているんですけど」 is being used to mean 「すでに指定席をとっています。何とかなりませんか」(We&apos;ve already booked our tickets. Can&apos;t you do something?).
はい。承知いたしましたが、場合によっては間に合わないこともあります。よろしいでしょうか。
I understand. We may not make it in time. Will that be okay?
「承知いたしましたが、場合によっては間に合わないこともあります」means: I under
s
tand what you are saying. We may make it in time, but there may be things out of my control, like
traffic, that will cause us not to make it. It&apos;s a polite way of explaining that there is a possibility of not arriving on time. Even in cases where you might think「多分大丈夫」(It&apos;s probably okay.) it is better NOT to say「大丈夫です」(It&apos;ll be okay.) in Japan. In this case, if the driver says 「大丈夫です」and then is not able to make it on time, it may prompt a bad reaction. The passenger may say &quot;But you said we would make it!&quot; and be angry. 「大丈夫です」is equivalent to a promise that the driver will definitely make it on time. So be wary of saying「大丈夫です」when you are not 100% certain of something.
まぁ、安全第一だから、仕方ないね。
Well, safety first. Can&apos;t do anything about it, I guess.
「安全第一」means safety first. See the Words from this Dialogue section.「仕方ないね」means there is no choice; there are no other options. In this case, the speaker is saying this to reassure both listeners that everything will be okay, and to defuse the situation.
昼ごはんでも食べるか。
Want to have lunch?
The 「でも」in 「昼ごはんでも食べるか」is being used to mean the same thing as 「例えば」. In the event that they cannot make the train, the speaker is inviting his young coworker to have lunch with him.
そうですね。
Good idea.
This phrase expresses agreement.
では、出発します。
I&apos;ll start driving now.
「では」is used to change the topic of conversation. It&apos;s a shortened form of「それでは」.

Words from this Dialogue
道路事情「どうろじじょう」
traffic conditions
「事情」is used to describe a status. Many things can be described using 「○○の事情」, such as 家庭の事情 (the situation at home), 身体の事情 (one&apos;s body&apos;s condition). 道路事情 refers to road conditions, or traffic conditions. This includes things like congestion or road work.
出発する前に、道路事情をよく調べておいたほうがいいですよ。
しゅっぱつするまえには、どうろじじょをよくしらべておいたほうがいいですよ。
It&apos;s a good idea to check traffic conditions before leaving the house.
就活事情「しゅうかつじじょう」The job-hunting situation
諸事情「しょじじょう」
Various circumstances
住宅事情「じゅうたくじじょう」The housing situation
安全第一「あんぜんだいいち」safety first
安全第一 means safety first. From driving a car to working on a construction site, this phrase can be heard in many different situations where danger is present. 「～第一」is used to mean that one thing is more important than anything else.
(職場のポスター) 安全第一、声かけ、確認を徹底しましょう。
「しょくばぼすたー」あんぜんだいいちこえかけ、かくにんをてっていしましょう。
(On a workplace poster) Safety first, reach out to others, and check thoroughly.
安全第一で今日も一日がんばろう。
あんぜんだいいちでもきょうもいちにちがんばろう
Let&apos;s do our best today with &quot;safety first&quot; in mind.
