## Cultural Notes

### Decision-Making Authority in Japanese Companies

In Japanese business culture, individual employees rarely have the authority to make major decisions independently. This is part of the ã€Œç¨Ÿè­°åˆ¶åº¦ã€(ringi seido) system, where decisions require consultation and approval from superiors.

When you can't make a decision on your own:
1. **Explain clearly** that you lack the authority
2. **Show respect** for the process ("I need to check with my supervisor")
3. **Provide a timeline** for when you'll follow up
4. **Never make promises** you can't keep
