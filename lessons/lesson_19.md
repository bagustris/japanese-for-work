## Cultural Notes

### Handling Customer Complaints Indirectly

Japanese customers often express displeasure indirectly rather than making direct complaints:
- Making observations: "The food isn't here yet"
- Sarcastic comments: "Must be nice to be busy"
- Questions implying criticism: "Are you understaffed?"

Staff must recognize these as complaints and address them seriously even though they're not direct.

### Omotenashi (ãŠã‚‚ã¦ãªã—) - Japanese Hospitality

The concept of ãŠã‚‚ã¦ãªã— in Japanese service:
- Anticipate needs before being asked
- Never make excuses
- Apologize sincerely for any inconvenience
- Bow when apologizing
- Use formal, humble language
- Take responsibility even for things beyond your control

### Managing Expectations Without Over-Promising

Important principles:
- Don't sayã€Œå¤§ä¸ˆå¤«ã§ã™ã€(It'll be okay) unless 100% certain
- Useã€Œä»Šã—ã°ã‚‰ãã€instead of specific time frames
