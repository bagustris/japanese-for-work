#11
Asking how to pronounce names
失礼ですが、お名前はなんとお読みするんですか。
I&apos;m so sorry, but how is your name pronounced?
お待たせしました。 Sorry to keep you waiting.
「お待たせしました」is a phrase that you use when you have kept someone waiting. To be more polite, use 「お待たせいたしました」. This phrase shows respect to the listener.
わざわざお越しいただいて、すみません。 Thank you for coming today.
This phrase shows the speaker&apos;s gratitude to the listener for coming to their location. 「わざわざ」means「忙しいのに時間をかけて」(making time even though one is busy). 「お越しいただいて」and「来ていただいて」are both acceptable to use here, as both are polite phrases.
本日はお忙しいところお時間をいただき、ありがとうございます。
Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today.
This phrase is being used by a person of lower status to a person of higher status. It&apos;s being used to express the speaker&apos;s gratefulness to the two listeners for meeting with him.
あ、早速ですが、ご紹介しますね。 Now, if you&apos;ll allow me to make an introduction.
「あ、」is an expression used to show that one has remembered something.「さっそくですが、」is a phrase that indicates it&apos;s time to move into the main topic.「ご紹介しますね」uses ご＋ます形 to make the verb more polite. 「ね」is used to show familiarity.
どうぞよろしくお願いいたします。 Hello, it&apos;s nice to meet you.
ファン・ソンミョンと申します。My name is Whang Sunmyung.
「（名前）と申します」is a 謙譲語(humble form) of 「～です」. When meeting someone for the first time, use 「～と申します」to introduce yourself politely.
どうぞよろしくお願いいたします。 It&apos;s nice to meet you.
When meeting someone for the first time and exchanging business cards, use 「どうぞよろしくお願いいたします」instead of「はじめまして」. Don&apos;t forget to make eye contact and bow to be polite.
失礼ですが、お名前はなんとお読みするんでしょうか。
I&apos;m so sorry, but how is your name pronounced?
When asking how to pronounce someone&apos;s name, 「失礼ですが」(I&apos;m so sorry, but...) comes first, as it may be perceived as rude otherwise. Add an お in front of「お名前」to be respectful when asking the name of a superior.
「なんとお読みするんでしょうか。」has the same meaning as「なんと読みますか」, but 「でしょうか」makes the question more polite.
「お読みするんでしょうか」and「お読みするのでしょうか」are both acceptable. The version with「の」is slightly more polite, but「ん」is also often used.

When confirming someone&apos;s name, use「～さまですね」rather than「さん」. Adding 「ね」on the end shows that you are confirming what you just heard. The subject,「あなたは」(you) is unnecessary in this case.
よろしくお願いいたします。It&apos;s nice to meet you.
どうぞおかけください。Please take a seat.
「おかけください」 is a more polite way of saying「座ってください」.「どうぞお座りください」has the same meaning. For a superior talking to a subordinate, use「（どうぞ）座ってください」.
Words from this Dialogue
失礼「しつれい」
rude・excuse me
「失礼」is a word often used in the business world. 「失礼」means &quot;rude&quot; by definition, but can be used in a variety of different situations to mean different things. In the business scene, 失礼 may be used to make a light and casual apology. For example, apologizing for not knowing how to read someone&apos;s name isn&apos;t terribly rude, but it may still be perceived negatively.
When you can&apos;t catch a caller&apos;s name during a telephone call, use「失礼ですが、もう一度お名前をいただけますか。」or「もう一度お名前を伺ってもよろしいでしょうか。」to ask them to repeat their name. Or, more simply, use「失礼ですが、お名前をもう一度お願いいたします」.
When leaving the office for the day, use 「お先に失礼いたします」or「お先に失礼します」.「さようなら」is not used at the workplace.「お先に失礼いたします」is used when you leave before others who are still working.

早速
「さっそく」
promptly, right away
「早速」is used when an action is being undertaken promptly. The meaning is the same as 「すぐ」, but 早速 has a more formal and proper nuance. When shifting a topic of conversation, 早速 is often used. In business situations, 早速 should be used rather than すぐ.
「早速ですが」is used when the main topic of conversation is about to be started. In other words, it means that the introductions and preliminaries are over, and the important information is about to come.
In emails, you may see the phrase「早速ご返信いただきありがとうございます」(Thank you for your prompt response.). In this case, 「早速」is used to thank the sender. This is a handy phrase can be used with superiors or subordinates.
早速ですが、今月の売上（うりあげ）について報告いたします。Now I&apos;d like to report about this month&apos;s sales.
時間に限りがあるので、早速本題（ほんだい）に移りたいと思います。Time is limited, so I&apos;d like to move on to the main topic.
