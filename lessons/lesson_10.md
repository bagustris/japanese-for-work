# Lesson 10: それなら、これを見るといいですよ。

#10
Offering support
それなら、これを見るといいですよ。
In that case, look at this.
すみません。 Excuse me.
「すみません」has many meanings. In this case, it&apos;s being used to get the listener&apos;s attention. You can use this phrase to get the attention of people you know, as well as strangers.
どうしましたか。 Can I help with something?
「どうしましたか」is a response to 「すみません」, and is a way for the speaker to ask what the listener needs. This phrase is useful both in the workplace and in daily life!
部長から、この書類を斎藤さんに渡すように言われました。
Buchoo asked me to give these documents to Saito-san.
「～（人、会社）から～（する）ように言われました」(I was asked to do [task] by [person].) is an expression used to explain that you have been asked to do something by a third party. The meaning is the same as「部長がこの書類を斎藤さんに渡すように言いました」, but has been rearranged to be in passive voice. 「言いました」is used to relay a fact; the focus is that &quot;someone told me something.&quot; When you are explaining that you have been asked to do something at work, use the passive voice like the example above.
「渡す」(to give) usually means 手渡し(to pass something by hand), and can&apos;t be used to say you mailed or emailed something. Instead, use verbs like these: 郵送する、メールする、郵送で送る、メールで送る.
どのフロアにいる人か分かりますか。 Do you know what floor he is on?
This question combines「（その人は）どのフロアにいますか」(What floor is he on?) and「わかりますか」(Do you know?). You can also use「どの階にいる人か、わかりますか」, because「フロア」and「階」share the same meaning.
Ex. 「駅はどの方向にあるか、わかりますか」Do you know which direction the station is in?
ああ。それなら、これを見るといいですよ。 In that case, look at this.
「ああ。」is a casual expression that shows that you are reacting to what the speaker just said. 「それなら、これを見るといいですよ」is a useful phrase for giving helpful advice. 「それなら」refers to what you have just been asked. 「～といいですよ」is used to give clear advice.
Ex. A:「買い物をしたいんですが」(I want to go shopping.)
B. 「それなら（買い物なら）、原宿がいいですよ。」(In that case, you should go to Harajuku.)「それなら、あの店がいいですよ」(In that case, I recommend this shop.)
Kanji: 見「けん」、
見解「けんかい」　view,
意見　「いけん」　opinion, 見る「みる」
これは全社員の配席表です。 This is a seating chart for the whole office.
配席表 is a seating chart that shows the locations of everyone in the office. Nowadays, many workplaces have seating charts that you can see from a computer or tablet. 全社員 means 社員全員(all of the company&apos;s employees). See vocab area.
これで確認できますよ。 You can check with this.
「これで確認できますよ」means「これを使って確認できます」. In this case, it means, &quot;You can check where that person sits by using this seating chart.&quot;
あ、斎藤さんは３階のこの席ですね。 Oh, Saito-san is on the third floor, in this seat.
「あ、」is an expression of mild surprise. By adding「ね」to make 「～ですね」it adds the nuance that you would like to confirm that what you have just said is correct.
わかりました。ありがとうございます。 Got it. Thank you.
「わかりました」indicates that you understood what was just explained to you. Add「ありがとうございます」to show gratitude to the person who explained to you. It&apos;s important to say thank you to build workplace relationships!
あともう一つだけいいですか。 Can I ask you one more thing?
You can use「もう一つ質問があります」to let the listener know you have one more question. However,「あともう一つだけいいですか」asks the listener if it is okay to ask them another question. Saying「あと」is optional. To be more polite, use「すみません、もう一つだけ質問をしてもよろしいでしょうか」.
取引先の遠藤さんにメールを送りたいのですが、連絡先がわからなくて。
I want to send an email to our client Endo-san, but I don&apos;t know his contact information.
This question combines several ideas:「取引先の遠藤さんにメールを送りたいです。でも、連絡先がわかりません。教えてください」(I want to send an email to our client Endo-san. But I don&apos;t know his contact information. Do you know?) .「送りたいのですが」or「送りたいんですが」are both acceptable.「の」is slightly more polite, but「ん」is used more frequently. Even though「連絡先がわからなくて」doesn&apos;t make a full sentence, through context the listener still understands that the speaker would like to learn the contact information.
それなら、部長に聞くといいですよ。 In that case, you should ask Buchoo.
遠藤さんと以前、仕事をしていましたから。 He used to work with Endo-san.
The subject of the sentence, Buchoo, is omitted. The full sentence could look like this:「（部長は）遠藤さんと」. The order could also be rearranged like this:「以前、遠藤さんと仕事をしていましたから」. By bringing Endo-san to the front of the sentence, the emphasis is placed on Endo-san. In Japanese, the word order in a sentence can be flexible, so the order of sentences can often be changed in this way.
わかりました。ありがとうございます。 Understood. Thank you.
Words from this Dialogue
取引先「とりひきさき」
client
取引先 means client. The character「～先」indicates a direction or place. 先方 is another word for client. 「客先(きゃくさき）」 can mean client as well, but it isn&apos;t used when speaking directly to a client; only use this word within the office.
新しい取引先のリストを作りました。I made a new client list.
客先から何か連絡あった？Have you heard from the client?
全社員「ぜんしゃいん」
all employees
The 「全」 of 全社員means「すべて」, or all. 全員(all members) is also acceptable, but when referring to all employees at a workplace, 「全社員」is better.
全店（ぜんてん）：すべての店all stores
全職員（ぜんしょくいん）：すべての職員　all employees / all staff
全営業部員（ぜんえいぎょうぶいん）：営業部のメンバー全員all sales department employees
