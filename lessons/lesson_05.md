# Lesson 5: 急ぎの書類はメールで送る、とのことです。

Relaying messages
急ぎの書類はメールで送る、とのことです。
She&apos;ll send the urgent documents via email. That&apos;s the situation.
お疲れ様でした。
Welcome back.
This is a greeting that can be used in the workplace. 「おはようございます」can be used, but 「こんにちは」and 「こんばんは」are not as common. In this case, it&apos;s being used as a greeting to a superior who has just come back to the office. When leaving the office for the day, 「お先に失礼します」and 「お疲れ様でした」are often used.
However, some workplaces may see it as rude when these phrases are used with superiors. It may be a good idea to listen to what colleagues say and copy them.

She got ill while you were out and went home early.
「お留守の間に」means「いない間に」(while you were out). It&apos;s appropriate for use with superiors.
「具合が悪い」means「気分が悪い」(not feeling well).
「早退する」means to leave earlier than the designated time. In the workplace,「早退」is often used in place of 「早く帰ります」.
伊東くん早退か。大丈夫かな。
Oh, he left early. I hope he&apos;s okay.
「そうか」is a casual form of「そうですか」.
「早退か」is a casual form of「早退（です）か」. The 「な」 in「大丈夫かな」shows concern and friendliness.
えっ、伊東さん早退?
Itoo-san left early?
「えっ」is an expression of surprise or remembering.
The meaning here is the same as「えっ、伊東さんが早退したんですか、本当ですか？！」and shows surprise.
いやぁ、急ぎの書類を頼んでいたんだけど、やってくれたかな。
Well, I asked her for some urgent documents. I wonder if she did them.
「急ぎの書類」refers to documents that are needed quickly! You can use 急ぎ＋の＋ a noun to say &quot;something urgent.&quot; For example:
「急ぎの電話」an urgent call
「急ぎの仕事」an urgent task
「頼んでいたんだけど」shares the same meaning as「頼みましたが」, but 「頼んでいたんだけど」 is more polite while still maintaining a sense of worry.
「やってくれたかな」can be used by superiors to subordinates, or among equals.
「～てくれましたか」is a more polite form of 「～てくれた」. Since the documents are ones that were requested from Itoo-san by Wada-san (coworker), he uses「くれた」, as in「（お願いした仕事を）やってくれた」.
伊東さん、そのことについて何か言ってなかった?
Did Itoo-san say anything about that?
「何か言ってなかった？」is a casual way of saying「何か言っていませんでしたか」. It&apos;s used by superiors to subordinates or among equals.
はい、急ぎの書類はメールで送る、とのことです。
Yes. She&apos;ll send the urgent documents via email. That&apos;s the situation.
「～とのことです」is a useful phrase that allows you to relay messages.
Just follow this pattern:「伝言(messages)＋とのことです」
Repeat what you were told, and then add 「とのことです」to the end. For example, If Yamada-san told you 「明日10時にまた電話をします」 (I&apos;ll call again tomorrow at 10.) , you can share this information with your superior by saying「山田さんは、明日10時にまた電話をする、とのことです」(Yamada-san said she&apos;d call again at 10 tomorrow.)
「書類は郵送します」→「書類は郵送する、とのことです」
I&apos;ll send the documents. → She said she&apos;ll send the documents.
「会議は11時に変更になります」→「会議は11時からに変更になる、とのことです」
The meeting time has been changed to 11. → He said the meeting time has been changed to 11.
「出張は9月10日から15日までです」→「出張は9月10日から15日まで、とのことです」
My business trip is from September 10 to 15. → She said her business trip is from September 10 to 15.
To tell about something that hasn&apos;t happened yet, use the plain dictionary form of verbs.
In this case, 「急ぎの書類は(これから)メールで送ります」(She said she wil send the urgent documents by email.) uses「～送る」. If the event has already happened, use the past tense.
To make「メールで送りました」 (She sent them by mail.) use「送った、とのことです」.

Words from this Dialogue
早退　「そうたい」
leaving early
早退 means going home before the regular time, or 「定時」. When asking superiors for permission to leave early, use 「早退してもよろしいでしょうか」「早退させていただけないでしょうか」(May I please go home early?)
When you know in advance that you would like to leave early, it&apos;s better to ask for permission a few days in advance. Asking for permission to leave early on the same day is generally only done under unusual circumstances or when you feel very ill.
Arriving later than expected is called 「遅刻」. Japanese people prefer punctuality. As a professional, it is a good idea to always let others know when you will arrive late or leave early. In addition, arriving early to work is called 「早出」and leaving late is called「遅出」.
早退してもよろしいでしょうか。May I please go home early?
早退させていただけないでしょうか。May I please go home early?
