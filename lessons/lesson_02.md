# Lesson 2: すみませんが、もう一度言っていただけますか。

#2
Asking someone politely to repeat something
すみませんが、もう一度言っていただけますか。
Would you please repeat that?
渋谷のカレー屋さんから電話がありました。
That Shibuya curry place called.
来週開店する渋谷のカレー屋さんか。
The one that&apos;s opening next week?
This is an abbreviated spoken form of「来週開店する渋谷のカレー屋さんのことですか」→ Do you mean the one that&apos;s opening next week?
This expression would be appropriate for use among equals, or from superiors to subordinates.
はい、開店が来月になったそうです。
Yes. Seems like the opening has been pushed back to next month.
The expression「～そうです」is used to relay information that one has been told. This sentence explains the fact that the opening has been pushed back to next month.
Rather than「開店は来月だそうです」, use 「なった」to create「来月になったそうです」. This shows that the opening date went from being undecided to decided, or that the decided opening date changed.
それなら記事のアップもリスケだね。
We must reschedule uploading the article, then.
「アップ」 means upload.　　「リスケ」 means reschedule.
リライトを合わせると、少しバッファーがいるね。
With the rewrite, we&apos;ll need some buffer time, too.
「リライト」 means rewrite. 「バッファー」 means buffer.
Depending on one&apos;s place of work, katakana words can be very common. In particular, IT companies and consulting firms use a lot of katakana words. However, using words like this too much can make sentences difficult to understand, so it&apos;s best not to overuse them.
ちょっといいかな。
Do you have a minute?
This expression is used by superiors to subordinates.
For subordinates talking to superiors, the following are appropriate.
「すみませんが、今少しよろしいでしょうか。 / Sumimasen ga, ima sukoshi yoroshii de shoo ka.」
「今、お時間ありますか。 / Ima ojikan arimasuka.」
The superior will say something like the following:
「いいですよ / Ii desuyo.」「はい、何でしょうか / Hai, nande shooka.」.
Now you can proceed with what you want to say.
この件は誰マターかな。
Who&apos;s in charge of this job?
This phrase uses the English word &quot;matter&quot; to ask who is in charge of a task
すみませんが、もう一度言っていただけますか。
Would you please repeat that?
Use this phrase when you didn&apos;t catch what the other person said. It&apos;s a polite way to ask someone to repeat.
There are often times where you can&apos;t quite understand what another person is saying.
When you want the other person to repeat everything they just said, say:
「もう一度言っていただけますか」「もう一度お願いします」
When you need clarification about the meaning of one part, say:
「～は、どういう意味ですか」「～は何ですか」
When you need to check just one syllable, use the Japanese syllabary.
For example, when you can&apos;t tell if the other person said &quot;Ishida-san&quot; or &quot;Hishida-san,&quot; ask:
「あいうえお、の『い』しださんですか」、あるいは「はひふへほの『ひ』しださんですか」. This is to check if it is Ishida-san, like the &quot;i&quot; in &quot;a, i, u, e, o,&quot; or Hishida-san, like the &quot;hi&quot; in &quot;ha, hi, fu, he, ho?&quot;
Alternatively, you can ask using words you already know:
「『いえ』」の『い』ですか」&quot;I&quot; like in &quot;ie?&quot;
Kanji: 言「げん」、言語　「げんご」　language, 発言「はつげん」comment, 言っていただけますか
この記事は誰マターかな。
Who&apos;s in charge of this article?
すみません。
I&apos;m sorry.
「すみません」is used in a variety of situations. In this case, this phrase is an apology for not catching what the other person said.
It&apos;s also being used as a way to get the listener&apos;s attention.
誰マター」とはどのような意味でしょうか。
What does &quot;dare-mataa&quot; mean?
Phrases like 「どのような」and「どんな」allow you to make questions more polite than just「『誰マター』とはなんですか？」or「『誰マター』とは何でしょうか？」
When asking for clarification, it&apos;s helpful to repeat what the other person said. This lets the other person know which parts you understood, and which parts you didn’t understand. It helps them to avoid needlessly repeating themselves.
誰が担当するか、ってことだよ。
It means, &quot;Who&apos;s in charge?&quot;
「～ってことだよ」is a casual form of「～ということですよ」.
それはカイルさんです。
That would be Kyle.
わかった、私からカイルさんに言っておくよ。
Got it. I will let Kyle know.
「言っておくよ」is a casual form of「言っておきます」.
Between people who are close, the phrase can be changed to「言っとくよ」. You use 「とく -toku」instead of「ておく -te oku」.
「伝えておく」→「伝えとく」 tell
「話しておく」→「話しとく」 talk
わかりました。
Understood.

Japanese often borrows words from foreign languages, often with little consideration for the original pronunciation or spelling. This can be a challenge for Japanese language learners.
In this case, Japanese 「誰」is mixed with English「マター」to create a new word. Words like this may be used only by certain groups or people in certain industries. Even some Japanese people may not know the meaning at first!
When you aren&apos;t certain of the meaning, be sure to ask for clarification.
