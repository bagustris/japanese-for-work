# Lesson 17: よければ、いつでも相談に乗りますよ。

#17
Supporting a subordinate
よければ、いつでも相談に乗りますよ。
If you ever need anything, just come to me.
ちょっといいですか。 Can I have a word?
When asking a coworker if it is okay to have a quick word, use this phrase. It&apos;s important to use phrases like this to show respect for your coworker&apos;s time.
和田さんのことなんですけど。
It&apos;s about Wada-san.
It&apos;s important to start off with what you would like to talk about. 「～のことなんですが」uses「が」in the place of「けど」to be more polite.
昨日もかなり悩んでいたみたいなんですよね。
It seems that something was really bothering him again yesterday.
「～みたい」is used to show one&apos;s perceptions or judgments. 「よね」is added to the end of this sentence to create a softer impression.
プライドさん、様子を見て和田さんに声をかけてみてくれるかな。
Praid-san, could you keep an eye on Wada-san, and reach out to him?
「様子を見て声をかける」is used to mean to keep an eye on someone and perhaps ask「どうですか。大丈夫ですか、大変なことはありませんか」or offer words of support if it appears they are needed.
はい、わかりました。 Yes, certainly.
部長、そろそろ行きましょうか。
Buchoo, shall we head out?
「そろそろ」is a useful phrase to let a listener know that it is time to move on to the next task. Phrases like 「そろそろ会議の時間です」(It&apos;s nearly time for the meeting.) and「そろそろ取引先の方が来る時間です」(The client will be here soon.) or「そろそろ行きましょうか」(Shall we head out now?) and「そろそろ帰りましょうか」(Is it about time to go home?) are key in prompting a listener to take action.
じゃ、頼んだよ。
I&apos;m counting on you.
「じゃ、頼んだよ」is a more casual form of「では、頼みましたよ」. It&apos;s a way for a superior to ask a subordinate to carry out a task.
はい、承知しました。
Yes, I understand
.
When a superior asks a subordinate to do a task, 「はい、わかりました」is an acceptable response. But to be more polite, use「承知しました」, the humble form of「わかりました」.
和田さん、作業はどうですか。
Wada-san, how is work going?
When talking with a troubled subordinate, use open-ended questions like 「どうですか」to give them the floor and create a more approachable environment. 「和田さん、何か問題はありませんか」is too direct, and assumes that there is some sort of problem. It is better to avoid an approach like this. Instead of「作業」, you can also use 「進み具合（すすみぐあい）」はどうですか」to ask how things are going.
はい、何とか。
I&apos;m getting by.
「何とか」means that although things are difficult, one is getting by. From this response, it is clear that the subordinate isn&apos;t doing well.
何か手伝いましょうか。
Is there anything I can help with?
By adding 「何か」in front of「手伝いましょうか」, you can ask if there is anything specific that you might be able to help with.「～ましょうか」is a verb ending that makes an offering of help or support.
Ex.
「手伝いましょうか」Shall I help with that?
「持ちましょうか」Shall I carry that?
「私がやりましょうか」Shall I do that?
ありがとうございます。でも大丈夫です。
Thank you. But I&apos;m okay.
The 「でも」in「でも大丈夫です」shows that the speaker strongly feels that they must carry out the work, because it is the work they have been tasked with. Depending on someone&apos;s personality, it may be difficult for them to ask for help or advice when they need it. A good leader must be mindful of these sorts of needs.
もう少し自分でやってみます。
I&apos;ll try on my own for now.
よければ、いつでも相談に乗りますよ。
If you ever need anything, just come to me.
「相談に乗る」 is a good phrase to use to a subordinate who may be struggling, or have too much work to handle. By adding「よければ」to the beginning of the sentence, the speaker leaves it up to the listener whether or not to ask for help or advice.
Kanji:
乗「じょう」、　乗車「じょうしゃ」 board a vehicle, 便乗「びんじょう」capitalize, 乗る
はい、ありがとうございます。
Thank you.
Words from this Dialogue
作業「さぎょう」
work, task
「作業」 is often used to mean the same thing as 仕事 (work) or 仕事をすること (working). 「作業」 differs from 「仕事」 in that it generally refers to a task with a specific goal in mind. Following orders from a superior falls under「作業」. It represents a smaller task, rather than an overall job.
作業中（さぎょうちゅう）working, in progress
作業服（さぎょうふく）work uniform
修復作業（しゅうふくさぎょう）repair work
声をかける「こえをかける」
reach out
「声をかける」 can mean to call someone or to strike up a conversation, or make an invitation. It is often used in spoken conversation. As a noun, it is called「声かけ」, and may also be used as「声かけをする」as a verb.
悩（なや）んでる部下に声をかける。Reach out to a troubled subordinate.
今度登山（とざん）に行くときは声をかけてください。Let me know next time you go mountain climbing.
