# Lesson 19: お時間を頂いてしまい、申し訳ありません。

#19
Dealing with ambiguous complaints
お時間を頂いてしまい、申し訳ありません。
I’m sorry it’s taking so long.
料理、なかなか来ないわねぇ。
Our order is taking forever…
「料理、なかなか来ないわねぇ」is a conversational form of 「料理がなかなか来ない」. The 「～わねえ」at the end of the phrase is used to add femininity. It tends to be used by older women.
そうだね。混んでるからかな。
You’re right. It’s pretty busy, I guess.
「混んでるからかな」is an attempt to find a reason why the order hasn&apos;t come out yet. 「～からかな」is a phrase used among people you are familiar with. 「混んでいる」is the standard form of the word, but here, the「い」is omitted to make the more conversational「混んでる」.
ちょっと、聞いてみましょうか。
Let&apos;s ask about it.
ちょっと、聞いてみましょうか。
Let&apos;s ask about it.
The「～てみます」in「ちょっと、聞いてみましょうか」means「試しに～する」, or to try something.「～ましょうか」is used when making an offer. 「ちょっと、聞いてみましょうか」is a phrase that a speaker can use to engage a listener and make an offer.
すみません。
Excuse me!
Here, this common phrase is used to call a waitress.
はい、ただいま参ります。
Yes, coming.
「ただいま参ります」is a humble form of 「すぐに行きます」and is appropriate when addressing superiors.
あの、「グリルステーキ盛り合わせ」って注文していましたっけ。
Uh, did I order the steak platte
r?
「あの」is used to show hesitation.「～って注文していましたっけ」is a conversational form of「～を注文していましたか」.「っけ」adds a more casual or familiar nuance.「グリルステーキ盛り合わせ」is a menu item that features several different types of meat on one platter. See the Words from this Dialogue section for more details.
確認してまいります。少々お待ちください。
Let me check on that for you. Just a moment, please.
「確認してまいります」is a humble form of 「確認してきます」and is appropriate when addressing superiors.
大変申し訳ございません。
I deeply apologize.
「大変申し訳ございません」is a very polite apology. 「どうもすみません」and「申し訳ないです」are often used when making apologies, but they may not be enough in some situations. When making a sincere apology, it&apos;s important to bow deeply.
注文は入っているのですが、もう少々お待ちいただけますか。
Your order is being made. Could you please wait just a little longer?
「～のですが、」is being used to politely explain the situation to the customer. 「～のですが」is slightly more polite than「～んですが」.「もう少々お待ちいただけますか」shares the same meaning as「もう少し待ってください」, but「もう少々」and「お待ちいただけますか」are more polite and thus appropriate when addressing customers.
そうですか…。
I see.
The 「か」at the end of 「そうですか…。」is being stretched out to show displeasure. A shorter「そうですか。」with a falling intonation has the nuance of 「わかりました。」. Please do pay attention to intonation and the lengthening of a sound at the end of an utterance.
そういえば、「にんにくのホイル焼き」もまだ来てないね。
Hey, we still didn’t get our garlic dish, either.
「そういえば」is a phrase that shows the speaker has remembered something thanks to something that the listener has just said. After realizing that the「グリールステーキ」hasn&apos;t arrived, the speaker remembered that the「にんにくホイル焼き」hadn&apos;t come either, and so uses「そういえば」.
そうよねぇ。今日は店員さんが少ないんですか。
That&apos;s right. Are you understaffed today?
The speaker is likely being sarcastic here. 「そうよねぇ」expresses agreement.「よねぇ」shows femininity. The meaning is the same as「そうだね」, but 「そうだね」 is more neutral and can be used between familiars with no regard to gender.
いえ、今日はあいにく混みあっておりまして…。
No, we&apos;re unfortunately quite crowded today…
「いえ」is a response to the listener&apos;s question. It&apos;s useful to use this when responding to a question, but it is important not to use it too abruptly. For information on「あいにく」, see the Words from this Dialogue section.「混みあっておりまして…」is a more polite form of「混みあっていて…」（混みあっています）(to be crowded). The ellipsis「…」at the end of the sentence indicates a pause, and regret. When in doubt, it is better to add「すみません」or「申し訳ありません」at the end of the sentence to show greater regret.
お客さんが多くていいですね。
Must be nice to be busy!
Based on the context, it can be inferred that the speaker is being critical here, and not complimentary. The same expression, when used in a different situation, may have an entirely different meaning. Even if it is said with a smile, it may not be meant in a friendly way.
お時間を頂いてしまい、申し訳ありません。
I’m sorry it’s taking so long.
This is a response to the previous comment「お客さんが多くていいですねぇ」. This phrase lets the listener know that the speaker feels strong regret about the long wait. Making customers worried or upset is not good おもてなし, so the speaker is apologizing for that.
今しばらくお待ちください。
Please wait a little longer.
「今しばらく」is often used in the business scene. It&apos;s a polite way to ask someone to wait a little bit longer, and is often used when speaking with customers or clients. In this restaurant situation, it&apos;s a perfect phrase.

盛り合わせ「もりあわせ」
platter, combo
「盛り合わせ」is a menu item that features several different types of food on one platter. It allows you to sample a little bit of everything. It can be hard to know just how much food a 盛り合わせ will have, so when in doubt, just ask. For example,「3人で食べますがどのくらいの量でしょうか」(Will this be enough for three people?)

あいにく「あいにく」
unfortunately
「あいにく」is used to communicate that something was unexpected and unfortunate. It includes a sense of apology, along the lines of 「すみませんが…」. It&apos;s also used to turn down invitations.
（電話で）あいにく山田は今席におりませんが…。
「でんわで」 あいにくやまだはいませきにおりませんが。。。
(On the phone) Unfortunately, Yamada is away from his desk right now.
（会う日を決める）申し訳ありませんが、その日はあいにく先約がありまして。他の日はいかがでしょうか。「あうひをきめる」もうしわけありませんが、そのひはあいにくせんやくがありまして。ほかのひはいかがでしょうか。
(When making plans) I&apos;m sorry, but unfortunately I have a prior commitment on that day. Are you available another day?
今しばらく「いましばらく」
a little longer
「今しばらく」shares the same meaning as 「もう少し」. The definition of 「少し」can vary from person to person. 「今しばらく」is somewhere between「今すぐ」and「しばらく」. It means &quot;not right away, but not too long from now.&quot; 「少々お待ちください」refers to a short amount of time. However, if one says 「5分お待ちください」, and then the action takes longer than 5 minutes, this will further irritate the customer. It is not common in the business world to keep customers or clients waiting, but when doing so, use 「今しばらく」. Adding a reason for the wait, such as in「準備をしておりますので、今しばらくお待ちください。」(We are preparing now, so please wait just a little longer.) is more polite.
在庫を確認して参りますので、今しばらくお待ちください。
ざいこをかくにんしてまいりますので、いまはしばらくおまちください。
I&apos;ll check to see if we have that in stock, so please wait just a little longer.
ただいま中村は接客中でございます。今しばらくお待ちいただいてもよろしいでしょうか。
ただいまなかむらはせっきゃくちゅうでございます。いましばらくおまちくださいでもよろしいでしょうか。
Nakamura is speaking with a client right now. Could you please wait just a moment?
